# RBI Canon

## Name

Rogue Baseball Intelligence (RBI)

## Core idea

RBI is a baseball intelligence and player-development platform for youth/travel baseball. It gives parents, coaches, athletes, and eventually organizations a practical operating system for making better development and game-management decisions.

## Product philosophy

RBI exists to help baseball people:

1. Protect athletes
2. Develop players
3. Prepare for opponents and events
4. Operate teams and organizations

RBI should support coaches and parents. It should not pretend to replace human coaching judgment.

## Core principle: athlete-centered system

The athlete profile is the center of the product. Every module should attach useful information to the athlete's long-term record instead of creating isolated data silos.

The long-term model is:

```text
One Athlete of Record
Multiple linked accounts
Many product modules contributing data
```

## Six-module product ecosystem

### 1. PitchGuard

Workload and pitcher protection.

Expected features:

- Pitch count logging
- Rest guidance
- Pitcher availability
- Tournament workload planning
- Pitching history

### 2. Pathway

Long-term athlete profile and development timeline.

Expected features:

- Athlete profile
- Goals and milestones
- Growth tracking
- Development priorities
- Long-term player context

### 3. PitchLab

Pitching mechanics analysis and drill prescription.

Expected features:

- Pitch video/mechanics review
- Mechanical checkpoints
- Drill mapping
- Practice recommendations
- Eventually connect to workload and athlete profile data

### 4. SwingLab

Hitting mechanics analysis and drill prescription.

Expected features:

- Swing video/mechanics review
- Mechanical checkpoints
- Drill mapping
- Practice recommendations

### 5. ScoutHub

Opponent scouting and game preparation.

Expected features:

- Opponent tendencies
- Game plans
- Tournament prep
- Matchup notes
- Roster/player notes

### 6. RBI OS

Team and organization operating system.

Expected features:

- Team dashboard
- Lineups and planning
- Player profile access
- Reports
- Coach/admin workflows

## Target users

### Parent/Guardian

Creates or manages youth athlete access, tracks development, and may own the athlete profile initially.

### Coach

Can be granted access to athletes and teams. Adds observations, metrics, notes, development plans, workload entries, and scouting context.

### Athlete

Eventually receives direct account access, especially for older players. Can view or manage appropriate portions of their own profile.

### Founder/Admin

RBI administrator with console access for reviewing users and athlete records.

## Product tone

RBI should feel serious, sharp, practical, and baseball-specific. Avoid fluffy wellness language or generic youth sports app positioning.

## Strategic direction

Start narrow. Make PitchGuard and athlete profiles genuinely useful first. Then expand into mechanics, scouting, and team operations once the core athlete record is durable.
